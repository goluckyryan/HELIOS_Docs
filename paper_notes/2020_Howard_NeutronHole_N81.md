# 2020 Howard et al. -- Neutron-Hole Strength in N=81 Nuclei

**Full title:** Neutron-hole strength in N=81 nuclei
**Authors:** A.M. Howard, S.J. Freeman, D.K. Sharp, T. Bloxham, J.A. Clark, C.M. Deibel, B.P. Kay, P.D. Parker, J.P. Schiffer, J.S. Thomas
**Institutions:** U. Manchester, UC Berkeley/LBNL, ANL, JINA/MSU, Yale
**Journal:** PRC (arXiv:2003.09299, Mar 2020)
**ANL authors:** J.A. Clark, B.P. Kay, J.P. Schiffer
**Experiment:** Yale tandem Van de Graaff + Enge Split-Pole Spectrograph (NOT HELIOS)

---

## Physics

**Context:** Systematic study of neutron single-particle hole states in N=81 nuclei
(one neutron removed from N=82 closed shell: 137Ba, 139Ce, 141Nd, 143Sm).

**The N=50-82 shell** contains 5 orbitals:
- 0g7/2 (l=4), 1d5/2 (l=2), 1d3/2 (l=2), 2s1/2 (l=0), 0h11/2 (l=5)

**Goal:** Track how single-particle energies evolve with proton number (Z=56->62)
using monopole shifts. Connects to understanding tensor force effects on shell evolution.

---

## Experiment

**Facility:** A.W. Wright Nuclear Structure Laboratory, Yale University (NOT HELIOS)
- (p,d) at 23 MeV -- good momentum matching for low-l (s1/2, d3/2, d5/2)
- (3He,α) at 34 MeV -- better matching for high-l (g7/2, h11/2)

**Targets:** Enriched oxide evaporated on carbon foils (~100-150 µg/cm2)
- 138Ba, 140Ce, 142Nd, 144Sm
- Oxygen contamination manageable (kinematics separate from isotopes of interest)

**Detection:** Enge Split-Pole Spectrograph + multiwire gas proportional counter + scintillator
**Normalization:** Elastic α-scattering at 15 MeV, 20 deg (Rutherford calibration)

**DWBA:** Finite-range DWBA (modern, not zero-range like previous works)
- Normalization from sum rules, not assuming ground-state purity
- Same potential approach: AK-style (An-Cai or similar global OMP)

---

## Key Results

**s1/2 and h11/2:** Most strength found in these two orbitals.
- s1/2: centroid energy well-determined, small fragmentation
- h11/2: high-l, well-populated by (3He,α), centroid tracked across Z=56-62

**d and g7/2 orbitals:** Significant fragmentation.
- g7/2 deeply bound -- much strength lies OUTSIDE the measured Ex range
- Summed strengths undercount total (implies more strength at higher Ex)

**Monopole shifts:** Centroid energies vs. Z compared to theoretical monopole shift calculations.
- Central + tensor components of nucleon-nucleon interaction needed to reproduce trends
- s1/2 centroid evolution well-described; h11/2 also tracked
- Demonstrates that proton-neutron tensor force drives energy evolution in this region

## Connection to HELIOS

- **Not a HELIOS experiment** -- Yale Enge spectrograph, normal kinematics
- **B.P. Kay + J.P. Schiffer (ANL)** -- directly connected to HELIOS team
- **Complementary to HELIOS:** Normal kinematics on stable targets + HELIOS in inverse kinematics
  on exotic beams -- together probe shell evolution from stability into exotic region
- **N=82 region** -- directly relevant to 0vββ decay candidates (136Xe, 130Te studied elsewhere)
  and to neutron-rich nuclei reachable by HELIOS beams
- **Same analysis framework:** DWBA (Ptolemy), same OMP potentials (An-Cai, KD), same
  sum-rule normalization approach used in all HELIOS spectroscopic factor analyses
- **Momentum matching strategy** (using two reactions for different l) -- same philosophy
  as HELIOS experiments that combine (d,p) + other reactions
- The systematic, consistent-approach methodology described here is the gold standard
  that HELIOS inverse-kinematics experiments aspire to match

---

## Key Lesson for HELIOS Analysis

**Deeply bound orbitals have fragmented strength** -- significant spectroscopic strength
can lie outside the measured Ex range, leading to underestimated summed spectroscopic factors.
This is a systematic effect that must be noted when comparing to sum rules.
