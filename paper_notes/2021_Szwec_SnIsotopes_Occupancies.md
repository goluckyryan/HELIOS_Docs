# 2021 Szwec et al. -- Neutron Occupancies Across Stable Tin Isotopes

**Full title:** Neutron occupancies and single-particle energies across the stable tin isotopes
**Authors:** S.V. Szwec, D.K. Sharp, B.P. Kay, S.J. Freeman, J.P. Schiffer, P. Adsley, C. Binnersley, N. de Serville, T. Faestermann, R.F. Garcia Ruiz, F. Hammache, R. Hertenberger, A. Meyer, I. Stefan, A. Vernon, S. Wilkins, H.-F. Wirth
**Institutions:** U. Manchester, ANL, IJCLab Orsay, TU Munchen, MLL Munich, LMU Munich
**Journal:** PRC (arXiv:2106.01848, Jun 2021)
**ANL authors:** B.P. Kay, J.P. Schiffer
**Experiment:** Munich tandem + Q3D (MLL) and Orsay (NOT HELIOS)

---

## Physics

**Tin isotopes (112 <= A <= 124):** The longest chain of stable, even-A isotopes between two
closed shells (Z=50 protons, N=50-82 neutrons). The N=50-82 valence space has 5 orbitals:
- 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 2s1/2, 1d3/2, 0h11/2

**Goal:** Systematic, consistent measurement of neutron occupancies and vacancies across
the entire stable Sn chain. Previous studies discrepant by 20-40%.

**Why Sn matters:**
- Longest stable closed-shell chain -- ideal for testing nuclear structure models
- 130Sn and 132Sn are key reference points for neutron-rich extrapolations
- Shell evolution near Z=50 is central to r-process nucleosynthesis
- Directly related to N=82 hole states (Szwec 2016, Howard 2020, Talwar 2017 work)
- The g7/2 orbital filling in Sn constrains tensor force calculations

## Experiment

**Facility:** Munich MLL tandem + Q3D spectrograph (primary) + Orsay for some reactions
**Reactions (on 112,114,116,118,120,122,124Sn):**

| Reaction | Probe | Matches |
|---|---|---|
| (d,p) | Neutron adding, low-l | l=0,2 (s1/2, d) |
| (p,d) | Neutron removing, low-l | l=0,2 |
| (3He,a) | Neutron adding, high-l | l=4,5 (g7/2, h11/2) |

**DWBA:** Consistent finite-range throughout. Same OMP parameters for all isotopes.
**Normalization:** Macfarlane-French sum rules, consistent across all reactions and isotopes.

---

## Key Results

**Orbital filling:** Figure 1 shows gradual filling of 5 orbitals from A=112 to A=124:
- 1d5/2 and 0g7/2 fill first, nearly degenerate at low N
- 2s1/2, 1d3/2, 0h11/2 fill at higher N
- **Key finding:** Below N=64 (A~114), 0g7/2 and 1d5/2 are ~2 MeV more bound than the
  other three (2s1/2, 1d3/2, 0h11/2), which are approximately degenerate with each other

**0g7/2 missing strength:** Significant fraction of g7/2 strength lies outside the measured
Ex range (deeply bound, fragmented) -- consistent with Howard 2020 findings for N=81 nuclei.
Treated as systematic uncertainty (hatched region in Fig. 1).

**Effective single-particle energies (ESPEs):** Determined from centroids of adding + removing.
- ESPEs evolve smoothly with A -- no dramatic reorganization within the Sn chain
- Centroid uncertainty reduced to few-hundred keV (previous studies: many 100s of keV)
- Comparison to theory: shell model + mean field reproduce qualitative trends

**Precision:** Occupancy uncertainties ~few tenths of a nucleon (significant improvement
over 20-40% discrepancies in previous systematic studies).

## Connection to HELIOS / h096 Area

- **NOT a HELIOS experiment** -- Munich Q3D, normal kinematics on stable targets
- **B.P. Kay + J.P. Schiffer** -- core HELIOS team
- **Same methodology as all HELIOS spectroscopic work:**
  consistent reactions + DWBA + sum rules
- **Sn chain is directly relevant to HELIOS physics:**
  - Neutron-rich Sn isotopes accessible via exotic beam reactions at HELIOS
  - 130,132Sn reference points for r-process shell evolution
  - N=82 valence space is the same studied in Szwec 2016 (136Xe), Talwar 2017 (137Xe)
- **Stable baseline:** This systematic work provides the stable-nucleus baseline against
  which exotic beam HELIOS results can be compared
- **Part of the ANL/Manchester systematic program:**
  Ni (previous work, Ref [23] in paper), Sn (this work), Xe (Szwec 2016), Ba (Szwec 2016)
  -- building a coherent picture of shell evolution near stability
