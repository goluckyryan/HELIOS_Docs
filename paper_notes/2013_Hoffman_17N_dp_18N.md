# Paper Notes: Hoffman et al. 2013 -- 17N(d,p)18N Single-Neutron Excitations

**Citation:** C.R. Hoffman, M. Albers, M. Alcorta, B.B. Back, B.P. Kay et al., Phys.Rev.C 88, 044317 (2013)
**DOI:** 10.1103/PhysRevC.88.044317

**Read:** 2026-04-28

## Physics

- **Reaction:** ¹⁷N(d,p)¹⁸N in inverse kinematics -- neutron-adding to a proton-hole nucleus
- **Nucleus:** ¹⁸N (Z=7, N=11) -- between ¹⁹O (ground state (0d5/2)³ J=5/2) and ¹⁷C (3/2⁺ g.s.)
- **Goal:** Identify neutron configurations of low-lying states in ¹⁸N; constrain (0d5/2)³ + (1s1/2) systematics in N=11 isotones (¹⁷C, ¹⁸N, ¹⁹O)
- **Structure:** ¹⁷N g.s. J=1/2⁻ ~ (π0p1/2)⁻¹(ν0d5/2)² → states in ¹⁸N are (p1/2)⁻¹ ⊗ (sd neutron)

---

## Experiment

- **Beam:** ¹⁷N (T½ = 4.17 s), 13.6 MeV/u, ~2×10⁴ pps, purity 25-75%
- **Production:** ¹⁸O⁸⁺ at 14.7 MeV/u on 15 mg/cm² Be → proton removal → ¹⁷N
- **Contaminants:** ¹⁸O⁷⁺ at 12.2 MeV/u, ¹⁴C⁵⁺ at 14.4 MeV/u (separated by recoil coincidence)
- **B-field:** 2.85 T (maximum HELIOS field)
- **PSD coverage:** -50.8 < z < -16.3 cm upstream
- **Targets:** CD₂, 140 and 220 μg/cm² nominal
- **Resolution:** ~275 keV FWHM (dominated by radioactive beam properties)
- **Calibration:** ¹⁸O(d,p)¹⁹O used at 14.7 MeV/u (primary beam) and 12.2 MeV/u (contamination beam)
- **Absolute cross sections:** NOT obtained (monitor detector noisy); relative only

---

## Expected States in ¹⁸N from 17N(d,p)

(π0p1/2)⁻¹ ⊗ (ν0d5/2)³ J=5/2 → J=2⁻, 3⁻ (ℓ=2 transfer)
(π0p1/2)⁻¹ ⊗ (ν0d5/2)²(1s1/2)¹ J=1/2 → J=0⁻, 1⁻ (ℓ=0 transfer)
(π0p1/2)⁻¹ ⊗ (ν0d5/2)²(0d3/2)¹ → above Sn

---

## Results

**States found:** E*= 0.12(1), 0.74(1), 1.17(2) MeV + tentative ~2.2 MeV

| E* (MeV) | ℓ | Jπ | GS | S |
|----------|---|----|----|---|
| 0.00 | 2 | 1⁻₁ | <0.10 | <0.07 |
| 0.12(1) | 2 | 2⁻₁ | 1.68(8) | 0.67(3) |
| 0.587 | 2 | (2⁻₂) | <0.13 | <0.05 |
| 0.74(1) | 2 | 3⁻₁ | 2.42(12) | 0.69(3) |
| 1.17(2) | 0 | (1⁻₂) | 1.44(29) | 0.96(19) |

- GS(2⁻)/GS(3⁻) ratio = 1.5(1), consistent with expected 7/5 for the J=3,2 doublet ✓
- Ground state (1⁻₁) barely populated -- consistent with seniority ν=3 (0d5/2)³ J=3/2 config
- 1.17 MeV: ℓ=0, assigned 1⁻ (or 0⁻+1⁻ doublet) -- this is the (d5/2)²(1s1/2) J=1/2 coupling
- **New state:** 1.17 MeV not previously identified

**Normalization:** N = (GS_ℓ=2 + GS_ℓ=0) / 6.0 = 1.49 (matched 18O(d,p) cross-check value 1.46 ✓)
Absolute uncertainty ±30%

---

## Shell-Model Comparison

Three interactions used: WBP, WBT, VMU(p-sd)
- VMU(p-sd) gets g.s. spin correct; compressed splitting between π0p1/2 partners
- WBP/WBT: better excitation energies overall, 1⁻/0⁻ ordering agrees with ¹⁶N analog
- All three reproduce general strength distribution reasonably

---

## Significance

- **Confirms spin assignments** for the 0.12 MeV (2⁻) and 0.74 MeV (3⁻) states
- **New 1.17 MeV state** identified -- the missing (1s1/2) partner
- Tracks (0d5/2)(1s1/2) neutron SPE evolution: 1/2⁺ state at 0.22 MeV in ¹⁷C → 1.17 MeV in ¹⁸N → 1.47 MeV in ¹⁹O -- dramatic Tz dependence
- Same Hoffman+Kay+Back team, same HELIOS setup as h096
- Good cross-check: normalization consistent between ¹⁷N and ¹⁸O datasets despite different beam energies
