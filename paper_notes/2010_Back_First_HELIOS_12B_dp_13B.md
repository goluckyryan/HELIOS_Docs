# Paper Notes: Back et al. 2010 -- First Experiment with HELIOS: Structure of 13B

**Citation:** B.B. Back, S.I. Baker, B.A. Brown, C.M. Deibel, S.J. Freeman et al., PRL 104, 132501 (2010)
**DOI:** 10.1103/PhysRevLett.104.132501
**Read:** 2026-04-26 (from HELIOS_Publications_Summary)

---

## Physics

- **Reaction:** ¹²B(d,p)¹³B in inverse kinematics -- **first HELIOS experiment**
- **Nucleus:** ¹³B, N=8 neutron-rich, p-shell near drip line
- **Goal:** Spin and configuration assignments for low-lying states
- **Method:** Angular distributions of (d,p) products, relative spectroscopic factors

## Key Result

- First- and third-excited states of ¹³B identified with spin/configuration assignments
- Good resolution demonstrated in first HELIOS commissioning experiment
- Established HELIOS as a viable tool for (d,p) in inverse kinematics with radioactive beams

## Historical Significance

- **This is the paper that launched HELIOS science** (2010)
- Commissioning paper: Lighthall 2010 (NIM A622) documents the instrument
- This PRL demonstrates the physics capability
- ¹²B beam from ATLAS facility; N=8 shell closure region
