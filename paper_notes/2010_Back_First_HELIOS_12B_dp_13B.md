# Paper Notes: Back et al. 2010 -- First Experiment with HELIOS: Structure of 13B

**Citation:** B.B. Back, S.I. Baker, B.A. Brown, C.M. Deibel, S.J. Freeman et al., PRL 104, 132501 (2010)
**DOI:** 10.1103/PhysRevLett.104.132501
**PDF:** `~/publications/2010_Back_12Bdp13B_FirstExperiment.pdf`
**Read:** 2026-04-26 (notes); PDF obtained 2026-04-28

## Physics

- **Reaction:** ¹²B(d,p)¹³B in inverse kinematics -- **first HELIOS experiment**
- **Nucleus:** ¹³B, N=8, p-shell near drip line; N=8 shell closure collapses in neutron-rich systems
- **Goal:** Spin and configuration assignments for the closely-spaced doublet at 3.48/3.68 MeV
- **Prior work:** Middleton & Pullen 1964 (t,p); Guimaraes 2000 (neutron knockout); Ota 2008 (12Be(α,t)); Iwasaki 2009 (lifetime) -- all gave inconsistent/incomplete assignments

## Experiment

- **Beam:** ¹²B (T½ = 20 ms), 75 MeV, ~10⁵ ions/s from ATLAS
- **Production:** 50 pnA ¹¹B + cryogenic D₂ gas cell → ¹¹B(d,p)¹²B at 81 MeV
- **Target:** 73 μg/cm² CD₂; B-field = 1.05 T
- **Resolution:** ~100 keV FWHM (vs ~300 keV from prior Si-array approach)
- **Reference reaction:** ¹¹B(d,p)¹²B used for efficiency calibration + normalization
- **Recoil detection:** ΔE-E Si telescope, 0.5°–2.8° (coincidence to suppress C background)

## Key Results

- 3.48 MeV state: clearly ℓ=0 → Jπ = 1/2⁺ (dominant s1/2 single-particle)
- 3.68 MeV state: ℓ=2 → favors Jπ = 5/2⁺ (d5/2)
- ℓ=0/ℓ=2 ratio for 12B(d,p)13B is 13±4 (exp) vs 1.84 (theory for 3/2⁺) or 2.94 (for 5/2⁺) -- theory underpredicts ℓ=2 strength by large factor
- Negative-parity states at 3.53/3.71 MeV: S < 5-10% of 3.48 MeV state (barely visible)
- Predicted 3/2⁺ state not seen below 4.8 MeV; may correspond to 5.1 MeV state from prior work
- Effective single-particle energies for 1/2⁺ (s1/2) and 5/2⁺ (d5/2) are too low in WBP shell model

## Historical Significance

- **First published HELIOS physics experiment** (PRL 2010, back-to-back with Wuosmaa 2010)
- Proves HELIOS can separate a 200-keV doublet that prior Si-array experiments could not (~300 keV res)
- Uses 20 ms beam -- demonstrates capability with very short-lived isotopes
- Commissioning instrument reference: Lighthall 2010 NIM A622
