# Paper Notes: Sharp et al. 2013 -- Neutron SPE Outside N=50 Core

**Citation:** D.K. Sharp, B.P. Kay, J.S. Thomas, S.J. Freeman, J.P. Schiffer et al., Phys.Rev.C 87, 014312 (2013)
**DOI:** 10.1103/PhysRevC.87.014312
**PDF:** `~/publications/2013_Sharp_N51_SPE.pdf`
**Read:** 2026-04-26 (notes); PDF obtained 2026-04-28

## Physics

- **Reactions:** (d,p) and (α,³He) on ⁸⁸Sr, ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹²Mo at Yale (conventional) + ²H(⁸⁶Kr,p) at 10 MeV/u on HELIOS
- **Goal:** N=51 neutron SPE evolution with Z (36-42) -- driven by tensor force as proton Fermi surface crosses fp→g9/2
- **Mixed approach:** Yale Enge split-pole (45 keV res for (d,p), 80 keV for (α,³He)) + HELIOS for ⁸⁶Kr
- **Motivation:** As Z increases from 38→42, proton Fermi surface crosses from j< fp shell to j> g9/2 -- tensor interaction sense reverses for high-j neutrons (νg7/2, νh11/2)

## Experiment Details

**Yale measurements (88Sr, 90Zr, 92Mo):**
- 15 MeV deuterons + 50 MeV α beams
- Targets: 182-328 μg/cm² enriched foils
- Fixed spectrometer aperture 2.8 msr; absolute CS via α elastic at 15 MeV
- Measured at angles targeting first maxima: (d,p) at 18°, 34°, 42° for ℓ=2,4,5; (α,³He) at 5°
- Resolution: ~45 keV (d,p), ~80 keV (α,³He)

**HELIOS measurement (86Kr):**
- ⁸⁶Kr beam at 10 MeV/u from ATLAS, ~5×10⁷ ions/s
- Thin CD₂ targets 50-75 μg/cm² (minimize energy loss contribution to resolution)
- Higher energy than previous 86Kr studies (5.5 and 7.5 MeV/u) -- better cross sections for high-ℓ
- Avoids complications of gas-cell windows (advantage of inverse kinematics)

## Key Results

- N=51 SPE systematics mapped across ⁸⁷Kr, ⁸⁹Sr, ⁹¹Zr, ⁹³Mo
- Evolution of νg7/2 and νh11/2 centroids consistent with tensor force driving changes
- At Z=36 (Kr, filling fp j< orbitals): tensor interaction attractive for j> ν states
- At Z=40 (Zr, g9/2 j> filling): tensor interaction sense reverses -- shifts in νg7/2, νh11/2 reverse direction
- New states found in 89Sr (2.961, 4.042 MeV) and reassignment at 3.42 MeV (ℓ=4 not 5)
- Consistent normalization across Yale + HELIOS datasets validates mixed approach

## Significance

- Demonstrates HELIOS used alongside conventional spectrometers for systematic studies
- ⁸⁶Kr measurement shows HELIOS advantage: no gas-cell window complications
- Part of the ANL-Manchester-Yale systematic campaign on shell evolution (connects to Kay 2011, Schiffer 2012)
- Otsuka (Tokyo) co-author -- tensor force theory perspective included
