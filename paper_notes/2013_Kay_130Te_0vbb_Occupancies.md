# Paper Notes: Kay et al. 2013 -- 130Te Neutron Occupancies for 0νββ

## Physics

- **Reactions:** (d,p), (p,d), (α,³He), (³He,α) on ¹²⁸,¹³⁰Te and ¹³⁰,¹³²Xe; also (p,t) on ¹³²Xe
- **Goal:** Constrain nuclear matrix elements for ¹³⁰Te → ¹³⁰Ba 0νββ decay by measuring neutron vacancies
- **Orbitals:** 0g₇/₂ (ℓ=4), 1d₅/₂+1d₃/₂ (ℓ=2), 2s₁/₂ (ℓ=0), 0h₁₁/₂ (ℓ=5) -- full gdsh shell
- **Beam energies:** (d,p)/(p,d) at 15/23 MeV; (α,³He)/(³He,α) at 50/40 MeV
- **Resolution:** ~30 keV FWHM for Te targets; ~60 keV for frozen Xe targets

## Key Experimental Details

- Te targets: self-supporting foils (436/671 μg/cm²), 99.2%/99.4% enriched
- Xe targets: **cryogenic frozen Xe** on diamond foil (~200-1000 μg/cm²) -- novel technique
- Normalization: Macfarlane-French sum rules; N = 0.57(5) for (p,d)+(d,p), N = 0.41(4) for (α,³He)/(³He,α)
- Consistent with Ni sum rule paper (Schiffer 2012): Nℓ=1 = 0.55(2)
- DWBA: PTOLEMY, finite-range; deuteron Reid WF; WS bound states r₀=1.28, a=0.65

## Key Result -- Neutron Vacancies

| Nucleus | 2s₁/₂ | 1d (sum) | 0g₇/₂ | 0h₁₁/₂ | Total |
|---------|--------|---------|--------|---------|-------|
| ¹²⁸Te | 0.72 | 2.06 | 0 | 3.34 | 6.13 |
| ¹³⁰Te | 0.50 | 1.45 | **0** | 2.21 | 4.16 |
| ¹³⁰Xe | 0.56 | 2.71 | **0** | 2.99 | 6.26 |
| ¹³²Xe | 0.26 | 1.96 | 0 | 1.77 | 3.99 |

**The key finding:** 0g₇/₂ orbital has **zero vacancy** in all four isotopes -- it is fully occupied and plays NO role in the ¹³⁰Te → ¹³⁰Xe transition.

The change in vacancy from ¹³⁰Te to ¹³⁰Xe is shared only between **d, s₁/₂, and h₁₁/₂** orbitals.

## Conflict with Theory

Both QRPA [Ref 29] and shell-model [Ref 30] calculations predicted a nonzero g₇/₂ role -- NOT observed experimentally. Theory also underestimates the d orbital contribution. This is a direct experimental constraint on the 0νββ nuclear matrix element calculations.

## Two-Neutron Transfer (pairing)

- ¹³²Xe(p,t)¹³⁰Xe: essentially all ℓ=0 strength to g.s.; excited 0⁺ states < few %
- **Conclusion:** For neutrons, simple BCS approximation is valid (no pairing vibrations)
- Contrast with protons in this region: (³He,n) shows BCS breakdown for protons

## Significance

- First systematic study of neutron transfer on ¹³⁰Te/¹³⁶Xe isotopes for 0νββ
- Companion to Kay 2011 (¹³⁶Xe SPE at HELIOS) and Szwec 2016 (full occupancy paper)
- Demonstrates that experimental nuclear structure data can discriminate between 0νββ models
- The g₇/₂=0 result is a hard constraint theory must reproduce
