## Physics

- **Reaction:** ²⁶Al^m(d,p)²⁷Al in inverse kinematics using isomeric ²⁶Al beam
- **Motivation:** ²⁶Al (t₁/₂=7.17×10⁵ yr) in ISM confirms ongoing nucleosynthesis
  - Low-lying 0⁺ isomer (²⁶Al^m) complicates astrophysical rate calculations
  - ²⁶Al^m is thermally decoupled from ²⁶Al ground state -- different destruction rates
- **Method:** HELIOS, ℓ=0 transfers to T=1/2 and T=3/2 states in ²⁷Al
  - Mirror symmetry arguments → constrain ²⁶Al^m(p,γ)²⁷Si resonance properties
  - First measurement of isomeric ²⁶Al^m(d,p) reaction

## Key Result

- Identified ℓ=0 states in ²⁷Al relevant to ²⁶Al^m + p destruction
- Mirror symmetry: ²⁷Al states ↔ ²⁷Si states (analogs)
- Improved constraints on thermonuclear destruction of ²⁶Al^m in stellar environments
- Demonstrates HELIOS capability for isomeric radioactive beams

## Relevance

- Shows HELIOS can use **isomeric beams** (not just ground-state beams)
- Transfer reaction selectivity: (d,p) preferentially populates specific ℓ states
- Same technique used in h094 (¹⁹F rotational states via isomeric ¹⁸^m F beam)
- Connects to galactic nucleosynthesis -- astrophysical motivation alongside nuclear structure
