**ANL authors:** R. Talwar, B.P. Kay, J.P. Schiffer
**Experiment:** Grand Raiden spectrometer, RCNP Osaka (NOT HELIOS)

## Physics Context

**The N=82 shell closure** separates the g/d/s/h orbitals below from the h9/2 and i13/2 above.
For 137Xe (N=83, one neutron above 136Xe N=82), the single-particle states of interest are:
- **νh9/2** (l=5, j=9/2-): above N=82, heavily influenced by tensor interaction
- **νi13/2** (l=6, j=13/2+): above N=82, high-j intruder

**Why it matters:** These states in 137Xe predict the single-neutron energies at 133Sn
(the doubly-magic 132Sn + 1 neutron) via isotonic chain evolution (Z=50->54 extrapolation).
The νi13/2 energy at 133Sn is critical for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.

**Previous work found:** Two 13/2+ states in 137Xe, but SF for the second one was unknown.
Reviol et al. (2017) observed the 13/2+_2 and proposed νi13/2 energy at 133Sn = 2366 keV
(surprisingly below neutron separation energy Sn).

## Experiment

**Facility:** RCNP Osaka, Grand Raiden (GR) spectrometer
**Reaction:** 136Xe(α,3He)137Xe at Eα=100 MeV
- Better momentum matching than (d,p) for high-l (l=5,6) transfer
- Also 144Sm(α,3He)145Sm for consistency check vs. 51 MeV previous measurement

**136Xe target:** Gas cell with PEN windows (C/H/O only -- avoids N/Cl contamination)
- Effective thickness ~850(120) µg/cm2, ~16-17 kPa, 298 K
- Two cells used during experiment (~1.4e16 total alpha particles)
- Absolute cross sections: normalized to optical-model elastic at 6 deg (~30% uncertainty)

**Angles:** 4, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21 deg lab (Xe); 6, 9 deg (144Sm)
**Resolution:** 65-70 keV FWHM

## Key Results

**Both 13/2+ states in 137Xe observed and quantified:**
- 13/2+_1: previously known, SF confirmed
- 13/2+_2: spectroscopic factor now measured for first time (previously only energy known)

**9/2- states (νh9/2):**
- Two states carrying most of the strength
- SFs reassessed from previous (d,p) and (α,3He) measurements
- Consistent with isotonic chain systematics

**Centroid energies vs. Z (isotonic chain N=83):**
- Tracked from Z=54 (Xe) to Z=62 (Sm) for both h9/2 and i13/2
- Tensor force evolution well-reproduced by calculations
- New data fill the gap at Z=54 (Xe -- only one measurable with gas target)

**Prediction for 133Sn (Z=50, N=83):**
- νh9/2 and νi13/2 centroid energies extrapolated from isotonic chain
- These predictions differ from Reviol et al. estimate based on shell-model fitting
- Better-constrained SFs for the 13/2+ states provide tighter constraint on 133Sn energies
