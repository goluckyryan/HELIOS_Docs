# Paper Notes: Tang et al. 2020 — First Exploration of Neutron Shell Structure Below Lead

**Citation:** T. L. Tang, B. P. Kay, C. R. Hoffman, J. P. Schiffer, et al., arXiv:2001.00976 (2020)  
**Title:** First Exploration of Neutron Shell Structure Below Lead and Beyond N = 126  
**Journal:** PRL (2020)  
**Instrument:** ISS — ISOLDE Solenoidal Spectrometer (CERN, sister instrument to HELIOS)

---

## Physics

- **Reaction:** ²⁰⁶Hg(d,p)²⁰⁷Hg in inverse kinematics (neutron-adding)
- **Beam:** Radioactive ²⁰⁶Hg from ISOLDE, energy above Coulomb barrier
- **Region:** Below Z=82 (Pb), above N=126 — almost completely unexplored
- **Motivation:** r-process nucleosynthesis path crosses this region; N=126 shell closure is the bottleneck responsible for the A~195 solar abundance peak

## Key Physics Points

- ²⁰⁷Hg (Z=80, N=127) — first spectroscopy of neutron excitations in this region
- Doubly-magic ²⁰⁸Pb is the cornerstone; moving away tests single-particle structure
- Near N=126 shell closure: level density low → statistical n-capture assumptions break down → specific nuclear structure matters for r-process
- s-orbital energies increase less rapidly than higher-ℓ states as zero binding approached
- Direct measurements of N=126+n nuclei "not possible for many years, if not decades" (2020)

## Connection to HELIOS

- ISS at ISOLDE uses same solenoidal spectrometer concept as HELIOS (ANL)
- Ryan Tang is first author — this is his PhD/early career work
- Demonstrates the scientific reach of HELIOS-type instruments: radioactive beams, r-process nuclei
- Same d(A,p) reaction technique used at HELIOS for stable/radioactive beams
