## Physics

- **Reaction:** ¹³⁶Xe(d,p)¹³⁷Xe in inverse kinematics at **10 MeV/u** (same as h096)
- **Nucleus:** N=83, ¹³⁷Xe; study of νh9/2 and νi13/2 single-neutron strengths outside N=82 shell
- **Resolution:** ~100 keV (90-130 keV range across PSDs)
- **Motivation:** (1) Extend N=82 isotone SPE systematics to Xe; (2) test HELIOS with order-of-magnitude heavier beam; (3) tensor force effects on high-j orbits

## Experiment

- **Beam:** ¹³⁶Xe stable, 10 MeV/u, ~5×10⁶ ions/s (limited to preserve CD₂ target)
- **Target:** CD₂ 125–175 μg/cm² (multiple targets used; lost ~50% after 5×10¹¹ ions)
- **B-field:** 2 T; 18 of 24 PSDs functional; 59 hours beam on target
- **Two target positions:** to cover ℓ=5 (θc.m.~32°) and ℓ=6 (θc.m.~40°) peak cross sections
- **Absolute cross sections:** measured via elastic deuteron scattering (Rutherford at 5 MeV/u)
- **Luminosity monitor:** elastically scattered C ions also tracked (monitored target degradation)
- **Background:** Xe+C fusion-evaporation protons -- smooth, subtracted
- **Proton cyclotron ID:** 32.8 ns period (2T), peaks also at 2T and 3T identified and cut

- Centroid evolution of νh9/2 and νi13/2 across N=83 isotones (Z=56–62) consistent with **tensor force**
- Fragmentation attributed to two-state mixing with core-coupled configurations
- Absolute SF uncertainty ~15% (dominated by monitor solid angle ~11%)

## Relevance

- **Same beam energy** (10 MeV/u) as h096 -- directly comparable kinematics setup
- **Heavier beam test:** 10× heavier than first HELIOS experiments, worked well
- Demonstrates HELIOS absolute cross section measurement technique
- Precursor to full 0νββ occupancy program (Szwec 2016, Freeman 2017)
- Target degradation method (tracking C/D ratio) is a useful technique reference
