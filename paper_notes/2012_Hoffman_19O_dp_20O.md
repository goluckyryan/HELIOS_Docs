## Physics

- **Reaction:** ¹⁹O(d,p)²⁰O in inverse kinematics; neutron-adding into N=12 oxygen isotope
- **Q-value resolution:** ~175 keV FWHM (limited by radioactive beam quality vs ~100 keV for stable beams)
- **States populated:** 8 with measurable strength up to E*~5.6 MeV
- **Orbitals:** 0d₅/₂, 1s₁/₂, 0d₃/₂ -- **same sd-shell as h096 (³²Si)**
- **Context:** O isotopes have N=14 and N=16 new magic numbers; N=20 gap expected to collapse near drip line

## Experiment

- **Beam:** ¹⁹O (T½=26.9 s), 6.61 MeV/u, 2-4×10⁵ pps, 50% purity (rest ¹⁸O at 5.69 MeV/u)
- **Production:** ¹⁸O + cryogenic D₂ gas cell → ¹⁸O(d,p)¹⁹O
- **Target:** CD₂, 260 μg/cm²
- **Two field settings:** 2.7 T and 2.0 T (different target-PSD distances to cover wider angle range)
- **Full 24-PSD array** (6 per side); proton TOF from 82.47-ns RF period
- **Recoil detection:** 4 ΔE-E Si telescopes, θlab ~0.5°–2.0°
- **Monitor detector:** annular Si, 2.4–4.8 cm active area, 13 cm downstream; deuterons at θc.m.=18°–24°
- **Zero-degree detector:** beam purity monitor (Ta foil attenuator 10³×)
- **Absolute cross sections:** via monitor deuteron scattering normalized to optical model

## Relevance to h096

- **Direct prototype for h096 ³¹Si(d,p)³²Si analysis** -- same sd-shell, same HELIOS setup
- Same team: Hoffman, Back, Kay, Schiffer (all h096/Hoffman 2026 co-authors)
- Shows resolution budget breakdown -- useful for understanding h096 ~300 keV resolution
- Absolute cross section technique (monitor + Rutherford normalization) is the h096 method
- Mixed-beam technique (¹⁸O+¹⁹O) analogous to h096 beam purity challenges
