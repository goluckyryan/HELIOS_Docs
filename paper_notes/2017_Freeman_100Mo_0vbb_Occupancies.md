# 2017 Freeman et al. -- Valence Nucleon Rearrangements in 100Mo Double-Beta Decay

**Full title:** An experimental study of the rearrangements of valence protons and neutrons amongst single-particle orbits during double-beta decay in 100Mo
**Authors:** S.J. Freeman, D.K. Sharp, S.A. McAllister, B.P. Kay, C.M. Deibel, T. Faestermann, R. Hertenberger, A.J. Mitchell, J.P. Schiffer, S.V. Szwec, J.S. Thomas, H.-F. Wirth
**Institutions:** U. Manchester, ANL/York, JINA/LSU, TU Munchen, MML Munich, LMU Munich, ANU
**Journal:** PRC (arXiv:1710.10817, Oct 2017)
**ANL authors:** B.P. Kay, J.P. Schiffer
**Experiment:** Munich tandem + Q3D spectrograph (NOT HELIOS), normal kinematics

---

## Physics Motivation

**100Mo -> 100Ru 0vbb decay** -- Q=3034 keV, good candidate for SuperNEMO, AMoRE, CUPID experiments.
Same framework as Szwec 2016 (136Xe) but for the Mo/Ru system.

**Key question:** How much do valence nucleon occupancies change from 100Mo -> 100Ru?
Large rearrangement inhibits the 0vbb decay rate (reduces NME overlap).
100Mo is near the Z~40 onset of deformation (N=60 shape transition) -- structural differences
between parent and daughter complicate NME calculations.

**Problem with existing literature:** Transfer data on 98,100Mo and 100,102Ru scattered across
many experiments with different energies, codes, approximations -- not internally consistent.
This work: consistent set of 4 reactions on all 4 targets using same equipment/analysis.

---

## Experiment

**Facility:** Munich tandem Van de Graaff + Q3D magnetic spectrograph (MLL)
**Reactions (all on 98,100Mo and 100,102Ru targets):**
| Reaction | Probes | Energy |
|---|---|---|
| (d,p) | Neutron vacancy (adding) | ~12-13 MeV d |
| (p,d) | Neutron occupancy (removing) | ~21 MeV p |
| (3He,a) | High-l neutron vacancy | ~28 MeV 3He |
| (3He,d) | Proton vacancy (adding) | ~28 MeV 3He |

**Why 4 reactions?** (d,p)/(p,d) best matched for low-l; (3He,a) needed for high-l neutrons
(same reasoning as Szwec 2016, Talwar 2017); (3He,d) for proton adding.

**DWBA:** Finite-range, consistent parameters throughout.
Same-reaction, same-analysis approach -- avoids systematic differences from literature comparison.

---

## Key Results

**Neutron occupancies:** Measured for 98,100Mo and 100,102Ru for the g/d/s shell.
- Occupancies in 100Mo and 100Ru show **significant discrepancies** with theory
- Same calculations used to compute NME fail to reproduce measured occupancies
- Pattern similar to 76Ge/76Se (previous Freeman/Kay/Schiffer work) and 136Xe/136Ba (Szwec 2016)

**Proton occupancies:** First consistent (3He,d) measurement across Mo and Ru isotopes.

**Deformation context:**
- 100Mo: near shape-transition region (Z~40, N=60 onset of prolate deformation)
- Structural differences between parent/daughter likely inhibit 0vbb decay
- Mismatch between theory and measurement suggests models missing deformation effects

**Sum rules:** Macfarlane-French sum rules applied -- consistent normalization throughout.
Some strength missing (outside measured Ex range) for higher-l orbitals.

## Broader Context -- The 0vbb Occupancy Program

This is part of a systematic program at Manchester/ANL measuring nucleon occupancies
for multiple 0vbb candidates:
- **76Ge -> 76Se:** Freeman/Kay/Schiffer (earlier work, references [5,6])
- **100Mo -> 100Ru:** This work
- **130Te -> 130Xe:** Referenced work [9-11]
- **136Xe -> 136Ba:** Szwec 2016

All show discrepancies between measured occupancies and theoretical predictions.
Collectively, these form the strongest nuclear-structure constraint on 0vbb NME calculations.

## Connection to HELIOS

- **NOT a HELIOS experiment** -- Munich Q3D, normal kinematics
- **B.P. Kay + J.P. Schiffer** -- same ANL team responsible for HELIOS
- **Same analysis philosophy** as all HELIOS spectroscopic work:
  consistent approach, finite-range DWBA, Macfarlane-French sum rules
- **Complements HELIOS:** Normal kinematics (stable targets) provides systematic baseline;
  HELIOS extends to exotic beams in inverse kinematics
- **100Mo is astrophysically/experimentally important** -- SuperNEMO/AMoRE/CUPID will
  eventually measure the half-life; nuclear structure constraint from this work is essential
  for interpreting any future signal
